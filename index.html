DA Lamp is a robotic lamp for makers or designers to automatically take pictures of every crucial step and upload to personal documentation database.

It is made of laser-cut frames, 3D printed components and casting servo arms. It is achievable and rapid to make DA Lamp from the open-source design in every makerspaces and fablabs.

DA Lamp has 5 degrees of freedom to move autonomously, and the master system is based on Android. The mobile phone can be mounted on the lampshade, and the built-in camera is capable of recording in the meantime.

The software is under development, including the framework of object recognition and interactive machine learning.

FabDoc

Dec 2015 - present

FabDoc is a web app platform for makers who are eager to document their projects step-by-step.

FabDoc merges with the concept of Git-like version control system. The commit-based workflow supports users to document in detail after the period of work.

To enlarge the scale of current open source hardware projects, FabDoc is under active development of the features, such as forkable branch, supporting teamwork and share.

Web App GitHub

In each vital step, the only thing you have to record is a photo and a commit while you are busy with making something.

After the period of work and recording, you are able to document more details in browser, and moreover, embed the step into your sites.

Rearia

2016

Rearia is a set of redesigned earbuds for the hearing aid users to enjoy the quality music over T-Coil mode.

My friend Yen-Tsen Liu started designing Rearia on account of his hearing impairment prohibiting him from having quality music anytime and anywhere. My role in this project is conducting research on materials, components, and T-Coil mode.

Rearia has successfully provided better quality sound. We are going to publish the instruction of how to make DIY version of Rearia.

BatDrone

2015

BatDrone is an app for people who wants to avoid crime hotspots and protect user on the way home.

The crime data was contributed by Open Data from Taipei City Government. However, the development of BatDrone is now suspended because the government has not maintained data in detail we need.

BatDrone is my first Android app, and this project started at the g0v hackathon in Taiwan. This project was also shared at the weekly meeting of Code for San Franciso.

hackpad GitHub

Push notifications will pop up if user turns on location access and approaches crime hotspots.

3D-tracking Abduction Game

2014 - 2015

3D-tracking Abduction Game is a low-cost 3D interface game of UFO abduction on Processing.

The original DIY 3D controller was made by kylemcdonald on Instructables with only three sheets of Aluminum foil and Arduino. On L3D cube with the 8x8x8 display, the user-controlled UFO and panic humans can be streamed by Processing over UDP.

This game is my first Arduino project.
